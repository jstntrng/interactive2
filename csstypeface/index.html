What is hiv and aids?


HIV is not spread easily, only certain body fluid from a person who has HIV can transmit the virus


HIV can spread through blood, semen, rectal fluid, vaginal fluids, and breast milk, not through casual contact.


HIV, Human immunodeficiency virus, this virus attacks cells of your body’s immune system.


T Cells

CD4 T-cells help your immune system fight off infection


HIV fuses to the t-cell and transfers it’s RNA


If left untreated the virus will continue to attack the CD4 cells.


When the CD4 count becomes dramatically low, AIDS is formed,

Prexposure Prophylaxis

PrEP is a medication approved by the FDA in 2004, otherwise known as Truvada.


The once-a-day dose has consistently been shown to reduce the risk of HIV infection by as much as 90 percent.


This has been especially beneficial to the people of San Francisco.

San Francisco

Is the leader of equality and LGBTQ rights but the city has had to carry the burden of HIV and AIDS for a long time.


Treatment has been transformative in San Francisco in the recent years. The nation’s onetime epicenter of HIV and AIDS is now the place where the epidemic can come to an end.


70% of all newly diagnosed people living with HIV are ongoing proper care after their first medical visit.


62% of people newly diagnosed with HIV achieve viral suppression within a year of diagnosis in San Francisco


The numbers are remarkable.
